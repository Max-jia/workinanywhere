The idea of working remotely during the World Cup sounds romantic. Laptop at a cafe in the morning, match in the afternoon, celebratory drinks at night. Wake up, repeat.

The reality involves a lot more logistics than Instagram suggests. Here's what actually matters if you're planning to work and watch football across time zones, countries, and questionable WiFi connections.

---

## The Accommodation Strategy Nobody Talks About

Everyone books near the stadium. This is a mistake.

Urban stadiums — Atlanta, Houston, Philadelphia, Seattle — are walkable from downtown and surrounded by actual infrastructure. You can stay in a normal neighborhood, work from a normal coworking space, and hop a short transit ride to the match.

Suburban stadiums — looking at you, MetLife in East Rutherford, Gillette in Foxborough, AT&T in Arlington — are surrounded by parking lots. The nearest coworking space to Gillette Stadium? *One.* And Boston itself is 30 miles away.

Here's the rule: **stay where the coworking spaces are, not where the stadium is.** Downtown cores have reliable internet, proper desks, and coffee that isn't stadium-priced. The match is 3 hours. Your work week is 40. Prioritize the thing you'll be doing more of.

If you're going to multiple host cities, book accommodation near transit hubs. Toronto's UP Express connects the airport to downtown in 25 minutes. Mexico City's Metro covers the central neighborhoods well. In Houston, the METRORail is $1.25 — essentially free compared to the Uber surge pricing you'll face on match days.

## Your Internet Backup Plan (You Will Need One)

I learned this the hard way. A cafe with "fast WiFi" during a normal week becomes a cafe with 80 people streaming match highlights simultaneously on game day. Your Zoom call at 2pm will not go well.

Here's what actually works:

- **Coworking space wired connections, always.** Not their WiFi. Their Ethernet. Most spaces in host cities have dedicated connections that hold up even when the building is packed.
- **An eSIM with backup data.** Airalo, Holafly, Nomad — pick one and buy a plan before you land. When the cafe WiFi collapses 20 minutes before your client call, you'll switch to mobile data and thank yourself.
- **Mexico-specific:** the average broadband speed is 43.6 Mbps. That's fine for email and document work. It's not fine for HD video calls or large file transfers. Book coworking spaces with verified speed tests, not cafes with "free WiFi" signs.

## Time Zones Will Make or Break You

This is the part most World Cup workcation guides gloss over. Here's the reality:

| Your Work Time Zone | Match Schedule Impact |
|---|---|
| European hours from East Coast (Toronto, NY, Philly, Atlanta) | Work mornings (overlapping with London), watch afternoons. Actually works beautifully. |
| European hours from West Coast (Vancouver, Seattle, LA, SF) | Zero overlap with Europe. You'll be working nights or missing meetings. |
| US hours from Mexico | Same time zones as your US colleagues. No issue. |
| Anywhere → Asia-Pacific clients | All host cities have terrible Asia-Pacific overlap. Prepare for early mornings. |

If you have flexibility in your schedule, the East Coast cities are the sweet spot. If you don't, accept that you'll be working odd hours and plan your match attendance around your non-negotiable meetings, not the other way around.

## How to Actually Afford This

Match tickets are expensive. Accommodation near stadiums is worse. Here's how to keep this from bankrupting you:

1. **Don't buy tickets for every match.** Pick 2-3 games max. Watch the rest at bars, watch parties, or coworking spaces. The atmosphere at a packed bar in the host city is often better than nosebleed seats.
2. **Mexico City is your budget anchor.** $3 beers, $12 meals, $45-65 daily budget. If you're hopping between host cities, spend more time in Mexico and less in the US.
3. **Coworking monthly passes are cheaper than daily passes if you're staying 2+ weeks.** Most spaces offer significant discounts for multi-week stays. If you're in Houston for the group stage, don't buy 14 day passes — get the monthly.
4. **Houston is the value pick in the US.** $1.25 transit, $30 day passes, $20 meals. Philadelphia comes close. Miami comes last — $47 day passes and the most expensive meals among host cities.

## The One Mistake Everyone Makes

They treat the World Cup like a vacation, not a work trip. Then their deadlines pile up, their sleep schedule collapses, and they spend match day exhausted and resentful.

Block your work hours first. Then fit matches around them. Not the other way around.

The people who thrive during this tournament aren't the ones with the best tickets. They're the ones who figured out their WiFi before they needed it, booked the right neighborhood, and treated their laptop as seriously as their match schedule. Be that person.
