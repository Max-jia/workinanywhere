You land in Lisbon with a US passport. You have a remote job with a company in California. You book an Airbnb for two weeks, fall in love with the city, and start wondering if you can just... stay.

The short answer: you can work remotely from Portugal for up to 90 days without a visa. After that, you need either the D8 Digital Nomad Visa or you are working illegally. Here is how it actually works.

---

## The First 90 Days

Portugal is part of the Schengen Area. Most Western passport holders, including Americans, Canadians, and Brits, can enter Portugal as tourists for up to 90 days within any 180-day period. During those 90 days, you can do what most remote workers do. Work from your Airbnb. Camp in cafes. Join a coworking space for a month.

Nobody from the Portuguese government will ask you what you are doing on your laptop. You are a tourist. Tourists use laptops. The 90-day limit is the only rule you need to follow during this period.

The problem starts when you want to stay past 90 days.

---

## What Happens If You Stay Past 90 Days

You cannot legally stay in Portugal beyond 90 days without a visa or residence permit. If you overstay, you risk fines, deportation, and difficulty re-entering the Schengen Area in the future. Border control systems are automated and track entries and exits precisely. You will not slip through.

Some nomads try the "Schengen shuffle." 90 days in Portugal. 90 days outside Schengen in the UK, Morocco, or the Balkans. 90 days back in Portugal. This is technically legal but logistically exhausting. Most people who try it do it once and then apply for the visa.

---

## The D8 Visa: What It Actually Requires

The D8 Digital Nomad Visa is designed for remote workers employed by non-Portuguese companies. The income requirement for 2026 is €3,680 per month, which is four times Portugal's minimum wage. You need to prove this income through bank statements, employment contracts, and tax returns.

You also need savings of at least €11,040, roughly twelve months of the minimum wage. You need a rental contract or proof of accommodation in Portugal. You need private health insurance valid in Portugal. You need a clean criminal record with an Apostille.

The D8 is an active-income visa. If your income is primarily passive (dividends, rental income, pensions), you need the D7 Passive Income Visa instead. The D7 has a much lower income threshold but stricter requirements about proving the source and stability of your passive income.

---

## Should You Apply?

If you plan to stay in Portugal for more than 90 days in any 180-day period, apply for the D8. The application process takes weeks to months. Start before you arrive. You can apply from your home country or from Portugal if you entered legally as a tourist.

If you are only coming for a month or two, do not bother with the visa. The tourist entry covers you. Spend your energy on finding a good apartment and enjoying pastéis de nata instead of filling out immigration paperwork.
