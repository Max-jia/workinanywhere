The World Cup is here. 104 matches across 16 cities in the US, Canada, and Mexico. The crowds are massive, the energy is electric, and if you're a remote worker who's ever thought *"can I just... go work from there for a few weeks?"* — you're not alone.

Digital nomads are flooding host cities. Not just for match day. They're arriving weeks early, settling into coworking spaces, and treating the World Cup as the world's biggest workcation. Euronews called it *"the largest temporary remote work experiment in sports history."*

But here's the thing nobody mentions in those glowing trend pieces: not all host cities are built for this. Some have 266 coworking spaces within reach. Others have 13. Some cities have affordable hotels near stadiums. Others will charge you $4,000 a week for a room within walking distance of a match.

I dug through the data from 20i's host city rankings, coworking availability maps, and cost-of-living comparisons. Here's where you actually want to set up your laptop during the World Cup.

---

## The Winner: Toronto (200/250)

If you can only pick one city to work from during this World Cup, pick Toronto.

The internet is blazing (200+ Mbps). There are 113 coworking spaces downtown. Canada's digital nomad policy lets remote workers with foreign employers stay up to 6 months without a visa — and potentially convert to a work permit. The time zone overlaps with Europe for about 3 hours each morning, so you can get your meetings done, watch a match in the afternoon, and still make happy hour.

BMO Field isn't the biggest stadium, but Toronto's infrastructure is the best of any host city. The city expects $3.8 billion in economic impact during the tournament. The coworking scene has been preparing for this.

**The catch:** Canada isn't cheap. Budget $110-140 CAD/day. But you're paying for reliability, not scenery.

---

## The Dark Horse: Houston (146.7/250)

Houston wasn't on my radar until I looked at the numbers. Now I can't stop recommending it.

$1.25 transit (cheapest in the US). 73 coworking spaces. 220 affordable accommodation options near the stadium. 178 Mbps average broadband. A day pass at a coworking desk runs about $30 — the cheapest among major US host cities. A meal out costs about $20. A beer? Six bucks.

NRG Stadium is an urban venue, meaning you're not stuck in suburban sprawl when the match ends. There are 16 coworking spaces within 3 miles. This is the sleeper pick for someone who wants to work, watch football, and not drain their bank account.

---

## For the Coworking Obsessed: New York / New Jersey

266 coworking spaces. Let that sink in. More than any other host city by a wide margin.

Work from Manhattan or Brooklyn during the week. Hop a train to MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford on match day. The density of cafes with reliable WiFi is absurd. If your idea of a good workcation involves being surrounded by other people who are also working, this is your city.

The downside: you'll pay for it. Meals run $25-35, beers $8-11. Accommodation near the stadium is scarce. Don't try to stay in East Rutherford. Stay in the city, commute to the match, and treat the workday as the main event.

---

## The Budget Pick: Mexico City

Mexico City is the cheapest host city by a significant margin. $3 beers. $12 meals. Taxis at $0.46 per kilometer. Your daily budget drops to $45-65 — roughly a third of what you'd spend in a US host city.

There are 171 coworking spaces in the city. The digital nomad community is established and welcoming. The 180-day tourist visa for many passports means zero paperwork.

**The critical catch:** internet speed averages only 43.6 Mbps — bring a backup connection and a VPN. Cybersecurity scores in Mexico are well below the 80-point "good" benchmark. This isn't a dealbreaker, but it means you need to be more deliberate about where and how you work. Don't handle sensitive client data on cafe WiFi. Use your coworking space's wired connection. A VPN is not optional here.

---

## The European-Time-Zone Play: Philadelphia & Atlanta

If you work with a European team, the US East Coast gives you roughly 3 hours of overlap with London every morning. Philadelphia and Atlanta both have urban stadiums — Mercedes-Benz Stadium in Atlanta has 53 coworking spaces within 3 miles. Philadelphia's day pass median is $32, the lowest in the US.

Both cities have decent internet (175+ Mbps) and reasonable accommodation availability. Philly has 465 affordable options near the stadium. If you're juggling morning meetings with afternoon matches, the East Coast time zone is your friend.

---

## The Wild Cards: Vancouver & Guadalajara

Vancouver has the second-best overall score (163/250) and internet speeds above 200 Mbps. The scenery is absurd. The Canada digital nomad policy applies here too. But time zone alignment with Europe is terrible — zero overlap. If you're an independent freelancer who doesn't need real-time collaboration, this is your spot.

Guadalajara is Mexico's dark horse. It's hosting 13 matches and has seen a surge in digital nomad interest, especially from Spanish travelers. Accommodation is more affordable than Mexico City, and the local government has been actively courting remote workers. Same internet caveats as Mexico City apply — bring a VPN, plan your workspace carefully.

---

## Three Things Nobody Tells You About Working During the World Cup

**1. Don't stay near the stadium.** Urban stadiums (Atlanta, Houston, Philly, Seattle) are walkable from downtown coworking zones. Suburban stadiums (Gillette in Boston, MetLife in NY, AT&T in Dallas, SoFi in LA) are in the middle of nowhere. Stay downtown and commute to the match. Your workday will thank you.

**2. Book coworking day passes in advance.** Spaces in host cities are filling up. Don't assume you can walk in on match day and find a desk. Book ahead, even if it's just a day pass.

**3. The real World Cup experience isn't at the stadium.** It's in the bars, the watch parties, the streets after a big win. Don't blow your entire budget on match tickets. Some of the best memories will happen at a coworking space watch party with people you met three days ago.
