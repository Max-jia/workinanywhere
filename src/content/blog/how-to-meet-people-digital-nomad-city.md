The first week in a new city is the hardest. You do not know where anything is. You do not know anyone. You go to a coworking space and everyone seems to already have friends. You go to a cafe and sit alone with your laptop. You wonder if you made a mistake.

Every digital nomad I know has had this exact experience. The ones who build rich social lives are not the most extroverted. They are the most deliberate. Here is what they do.

---

## Book a Coliving Space for Your First Week

This is the single highest-return decision you can make. A coliving space like Outsite, Selina, or a local option forces social interaction in a way that renting an Airbnb does not. You share a kitchen. You run into people at breakfast. Someone invites you to a group dinner on your second night, and suddenly you know five people.

Coliving costs more than an Airbnb. For one week, it is worth it. You are not paying for the room. You are paying for an instant social circle that removes the hardest part of arriving somewhere alone. Move to a cheaper apartment in week two after you have already met people.

---

## Go to the Coworking Events, Even the Boring Ones

Every coworking space has events. Trivia night. Friday drinks. Yoga Wednesday. Most of them sound boring. Go anyway. The people who attend these events are the people who want to meet other people. They are your target demographic.

Say yes to every social invitation in your first ten days. Lunch after coworking. Group dinner. A weekend trip to a waterfall someone is organizing. Even if you are tired. Even if you would rather stay in. The window for integrating into a social circle is short. Front-load your social effort.

---

## Use the Group Chats Before You Arrive

Every nomad hub has WhatsApp and Telegram groups. Search "[city] digital nomads" or "[city] expats." Join them before you land. Post a message. "Arriving on the 15th, working remotely in tech, staying for two months. Anyone around for coffee?" This is normal. Everyone in the group joined for exactly this reason. You will not look desperate. You will look like someone who understands how this works.

---

## The One Habit That Changes Everything

Be the person who organizes things. Most people in nomad communities want to be social but will not take the initiative. If you send a message in the group chat that says "grabbing coffee at 3pm at this cafe, anyone want to join," people will show up. If you organize a weekly dinner, people will come. If you become the person who makes plans, people will start inviting you to theirs.

You do not need to be extroverted to do this. You need to send one message. The social return on that effort is enormous.
