The freelance platform you choose directly impacts how much of your earnings you keep. A 20% commission on a $5,000 project is $1,000 — that's real money. Yet many freelancers pick a platform based on name recognition alone, without comparing the numbers.

Here's how the four major platforms compare in 2026.

---

## Fee Comparison

| Platform | Freelancer Fee | Client Fee |
|---|---|---|
| **Upwork** | 10% flat (simplified from old sliding scale) | 5–10% |
| **Fiverr** | 20% on every transaction | 5.5% + $3.50 on orders under $100 |
| **Toptal** | 0% to freelancers | Undisclosed markup (reportedly up to 50%) + $79/mo subscription + $500 deposit |
| **Contra** | 0% commission | Free |

The difference on meaningful projects is dramatic. On a $5,000 project: Upwork takes $500. Fiverr takes $1,000. Contra and Toptal take $0 from you — though for very different reasons (Contra monetizes through optional Pro subscriptions; Toptal charges clients more instead).

---

## Platform-by-Platform Breakdown

### Upwork — The Generalist Giant

- **18 million+ freelancers**, $4.3 billion annual billings
- Best for long-term contracts, hourly and fixed-price projects, broad skill categories
- Escrow payment protection on hourly and fixed-price contracts
- New freelancers need "Connects" credits to apply (small cost per proposal)
- Typical rates: $15–150/hr depending on skill and experience
- **Best for:** Freelancers who want the largest pool of clients and are comfortable competing on proposals

**Watch for:** The 10% flat fee replaced the old sliding scale (which was 20%/10%/5%). While it's simpler, it's still 10% of everything you earn — including long-term contracts with repeat clients.

---

### Fiverr — The Gig Marketplace

- Gig-based: you create service listings, clients buy them
- Best for quick, clearly defined, one-off deliverables
- Massive buyer traffic but quality varies dramatically
- Payment held for 14 days (7 days for top-rated sellers)
- Fiverr Pro vets top 1% of talent for higher-quality projects
- **Best for:** Freelancers selling clearly packaged services at fixed prices

**Watch for:** The 20% commission is the highest in the industry. On a $100 gig, you keep $80. On small-ticket work this adds up fast. Fiverr works best when you can standardize your offering and earn through volume or premium-priced Pro gigs.

---

### Toptal — The Elite Screening Platform

- Accepts only top 3% of applicants through a rigorous 5-step screening
- 0% freelancer commission — you set your rate and keep all of it
- Clients: enterprises, funded startups, mission-critical projects
- 98% trial-to-hire success rate
- Typical freelancer rates: $60–200+/hr
- **Best for:** Senior developers, designers, and finance experts who can pass the screening

**Watch for:** The screening process is genuinely hard — most applicants don't pass. Client pricing is opaque: a client might pay $150/hr for a developer who charges $100/hr. If you can get in, the project quality and compensation are excellent. But it's not a platform you casually sign up for.

---

### Contra — The Zero-Commission Challenger

- Portfolio-first, modern interface
- 0% commission for freelancers (monetizes via Contra Pro at $20/month)
- Growing rapidly as an alternative to Upwork/Fiverr
- Younger platform — smaller talent pool, fewer job listings
- Minimum payout: $25
- **Best for:** Freelancers who want to keep 100% of their earnings and build a portfolio-driven presence

**Watch for:** Smaller client base means fewer inbound leads. You'll need to drive your own traffic to your Contra profile or supplement with other platforms. The zero-commission model is appealing but only works if clients are actually there.

---

## Which Platform Should You Use?

| Your situation | Best platform |
|---|---|
| Starting out, building a client base | **Upwork** — largest pool of clients |
| Selling standardized services at fixed prices | **Fiverr** — gig-based model |
| Senior-level, high-value projects | **Toptal** — if you can pass the screening |
| Established freelancer tired of fees | **Contra** — zero commission |
| Want maximum coverage | **Upwork + Contra** (active + passive) |

---

## One More Thing

The best freelance platform is the one your clients already use — but the best long-term strategy is to build a client base that doesn't depend on any platform. Every fee you pay to a marketplace is a fee you won't pay once you have direct relationships and referrals.

Use platforms to get started and build your portfolio and reputation. Transition to direct clients as soon as you have the momentum to do so.
