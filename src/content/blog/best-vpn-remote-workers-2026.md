I've been using VPNs from cafe tables and coworking desks across six countries. Here's what actually works, based on hands-on testing and the latest independent benchmarks from PCMag, Gizmodo, and The Independent.

## NordVPN — The One I Recommend Most

This is the one I use personally. Not because it's the cheapest (it isn't) or the fastest in every test (it's close), but because it does everything well enough that I never have to think about it. That's the real test of a VPN for a nomad — does it fade into the background and just work?

It does. The NordLynx protocol is fast — PCMag's testing showed only a 5.2% download speed loss, and the latency hit is the lowest among the four at 58%. Their server network is massive (9,400+ servers in 137 countries), so you're rarely stuck on an overloaded connection. Threat Protection Pro blocks ads and trackers at the network level, which means fewer distractions and faster page loads even without an ad blocker installed.

**The one thing that matters for nomads:** NordVPN has been independently audited six times by Deloitte, most recently in 2026. Their no-logs policy isn't marketing — it's been verified. If you're working from countries where internet freedom isn't guaranteed, this matters.

**$3.09/month** on a 2-year plan. Not the cheapest, but the best balance of speed, security, and reliability.

## Surfshark — Best for Budget Nomads

If you're just starting out and every dollar counts, get Surfshark. At $1.99/month it's the cheapest of the four, and PCMag's speed tests actually showed the *fastest* download speeds — only a 3.25% loss. That's impressive for a budget option.

The killer feature for nomads with multiple devices: **unlimited simultaneous connections.** Most VPNs cap you at 10 devices. Surfshark doesn't care — connect your laptop, phone, tablet, and whatever else you're carrying, all at once.

The trade-off: renewal prices are significantly higher (most VPNs do this, but Surfshark's jump is notable). CleanWeb ad blocking works well but can be inconsistent. And their app interface isn't as polished as ExpressVPN's.

**$1.99/month** on a 2-year plan. Best value among the four.

## ExpressVPN — Best If You've Never Used a VPN Before

ExpressVPN has the cleanest app of any VPN I've tested. If you're setting up a VPN for the first time and don't want to think about protocols, kill switches, or split tunneling — get ExpressVPN. It just works, and the interface is genuinely intuitive.

Their Lightway protocol is fast (PCMag showed 18.55% speed loss — not the fastest, but completely usable) and designed for switching between WiFi and mobile networks without dropping the connection. For nomads who are constantly moving between coworking spaces, cafes, and accommodation, this reliability matters more than raw speed.

The downside: fewer advanced features. No double VPN. No multi-hop. If you're a power user who wants to configure everything, ExpressVPN will feel limiting. And at $2.79/month, it's not cheap for what you get.

**$2.79/month** on a 2-year plan. Best for beginners who value simplicity.

## ProtonVPN — Best for Privacy

If you're a journalist, an activist, or just someone who takes digital privacy seriously, ProtonVPN is the one. It's fully open-source, based in Switzerland (strong privacy laws), and operated by the same team behind Proton Mail. Every server runs full-disk encryption.

The free tier is genuinely useful — unlimited data, no ads. Most free VPNs are selling your data. ProtonVPN's free tier is funded by its paid users. If you genuinely can't afford a VPN right now, use ProtonVPN Free rather than downloading something random from an app store.

The downside: speed. PCMag showed a 20% download loss and latency increases that are notably higher than the competition. Their "VPN Accelerator" feature helps close the gap, but Proton is not the fastest option for streaming or large file transfers.

**Free** (unlimited, no ads) or **$2.99/month** for paid. Best for privacy-conscious users.

## What About Speed?

Here's what PCMag's 2026 tests found — the percentage of speed lost when connected to each VPN:

| | Download Loss | Upload Loss | Latency Increase |
|---|---|---|---|
| Surfshark | 3.25% | 6.07% | 83.92% |
| NordVPN | 5.20% | 5.78% | 58.14% |
| ExpressVPN | 18.55% | 7.45% | 95.62% |
| ProtonVPN | 20.11% | 43.21% | 351.26% |

**What this actually means:** On a 100 Mbps connection, NordVPN and Surfshark give you ~95 Mbps. ExpressVPN gives you ~81 Mbps. ProtonVPN gives you ~80 Mbps. For 99% of remote work — video calls, file uploads, streaming — any of these is fast enough. The differences only matter for very large file transfers or competitive gaming.
