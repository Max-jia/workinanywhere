FlexJobs released their 2026 digital nomad report last month, and one number stopped me: **36% of Americans said they would move to a new city, state, or country if they had a work-from-anywhere job.** Another 17% said they would travel frequently as a digital nomad. That is more than half the workforce.

The demand is there. The question is whether the jobs are. Based on FlexJobs' data from January to May 2026, here's who's actually hiring.

---

## The Top 10 Work-From-Anywhere Companies

These companies had the most work-from-anywhere job listings in the first half of 2026:

1. **Wikimedia Foundation** — The nonprofit behind Wikipedia. Fully distributed, async-first culture. If your idea of meaningful work involves making knowledge free, this is it.
2. **Xapo Bank** — Crypto-native bank. Fully remote, hires globally. Competitive comp, unusual for a financial services company.
3. **Supabase** — Open-source Firebase alternative. Remote-first, engineering-heavy, transparent culture. Their CEO tweets about company metrics publicly.
4. **Railway** — Platform-as-a-service startup. Fast-growing, remote-first, appeals to developers who like infrastructure.
5. **Eliassen Group** — IT staffing and consulting. Less flashy than the startups on this list, but stable and consistently hiring remote roles.
6. **G2i** — Developer talent platform. Work-from-anywhere roles for engineers, focused on matching talent with startups.
7. **Invisible Technologies** — Operations and process automation. Quietly one of the largest fully remote companies. Hires globally across operations, engineering, and leadership.
8. **Superside** — Creative-as-a-service platform. Fully remote creative teams. Designers, project managers, and account roles.
9. **Cloudlinux** — Enterprise Linux OS. Remote-first, hires globally for engineering and support roles.
10. **Appinio** — Market research platform. Remote across Europe and the Americas.

## The Hottest Roles Right Now

FlexJobs tracked the most in-demand remote positions. If you're wondering what to skill up in, start here:

- Customer Success Manager
- Executive Assistant
- Data Scientist
- Software Engineer
- Social Media Manager
- Operations Manager
- Account Manager
- Visual Designer
- Data Engineer
- Product Manager

The range is wider than most people think. It's not just developers. Companies need people who can write, manage, design, and communicate — and they increasingly don't care where those people sit.

---

## What This Means for Your Job Hunt

The competition for remote jobs in 2026 is genuinely different from 2020. Companies are not scrambling to figure out remote work anymore. They've figured it out. The ones that have committed to work-from-anywhere have done so deliberately — and they filter for people who already know how to work remotely.

Demonstrate remote competence in your application. Mention your home office setup. Your experience with async tools. Your time zone flexibility. Don't make a hiring manager guess whether you can handle remote work. Show them you already have.

The companies on this list aren't doing you a favor by letting you work remotely. They've built their entire operations around distributed teams. Apply like you understand that.
