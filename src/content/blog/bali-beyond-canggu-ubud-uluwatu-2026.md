Canggu is the default answer when someone says they're working remotely from Bali. It has the highest concentration of coworking spaces, cafes, and nomads on the island. But it's also crowded, increasingly expensive, and not everyone's idea of paradise — the traffic alone has driven many nomads to explore alternatives.

Here's a practical guide to Bali's different regions in 2026, for different kinds of remote workers.

---

## Canggu — The Social Hub

**Best for:** First-time Bali nomads, social butterflies, surfers

This is where most people land, and for good reason. The density of coworking spaces, cafes, gyms, and restaurants in Canggu is unmatched on the island. You can walk or scooter to everything you need. There's always something happening — events, workshops, group dinners, surf meetups.

**The trade-off:** Traffic is genuinely bad. What looks like a 10-minute drive on the map can take 40 minutes at peak hours. The main roads (Batu Bolong, Berawa, Pererenan) are gridlocked during rush hour. Rents have risen roughly 35% since 2022 as demand has surged.

**Monthly budget (comfortable):** $1,400–2,200

---

## Ubud — The Wellness & Focus Destination

**Best for:** Yoga, meditation, creative work, deep focus, wellness retreats

Ubud is Bali's cultural and spiritual center. It's surrounded by rice terraces, temples, and jungle. The pace is slower. The food scene is outstanding — from $2 local warungs to high-end vegan restaurants. Coworking spaces like Outpost and Hubud attract a quieter, more focused crowd than Canggu's social scene.

**The trade-off:** Further from the beach (30–45 minutes by scooter). Quieter nightlife. More spread out — you'll need a scooter. Some find it too quiet after a few weeks.

**Monthly budget:** $1,200–2,000

---

## Uluwatu — The Surfer's Paradise

**Best for:** Surfers, sunset chasers, those who prioritize ocean over coworking

Uluwatu sits on the Bukit Peninsula's southern cliffs, with dramatic ocean views and some of the best surf breaks in the world. The vibe is more laid-back than Canggu, with a strong surf and wellness culture. The coworking scene is smaller but growing — spaces like Dojo Bali and Kinship Studio serve the remote work crowd.

**The trade-off:** Much more spread out than Canggu — you'll need a scooter for everything. Fewer coworking options and cafes. Less social infrastructure for meeting people outside of surf culture. Not ideal if you want a dense nomad community.

**Monthly budget:** $1,300–2,000

---

## Sanur — The Underrated Alternative

**Best for:** Older nomads, families, those who want calm with infrastructure

Sanur is on the east coast, away from the Canggu buzz. It's quieter, more residential, and has a calm beach with a paved path that runs for kilometers along the coast — ideal for running, walking, and cycling. The coworking scene is small but functional (BWork Bali, Kevala). The expat community is older and more settled.

**The trade-off:** Much quieter nightlife. Limited coworking options. Further from the Canggu/Ubud social scenes. Not the place if you want a dense nomad social life.

**Monthly budget:** $1,200–1,800

---

## Sidemen / Amed / North Bali — The Escape

**Best for:** Deep work retreats, short getaways, people who genuinely want isolation

These areas are not practical as your primary base — you'll be far from coworking spaces, reliable infrastructure, and other nomads. But for a week or two of deep work with a view of Mount Agung, Sidemen's rice terraces deliver.

## How to Approach Bali

Don't commit to one area for your entire stay before arriving. Spend your first week in Canggu to meet people and get oriented. Then split your remaining time between one or two other areas based on what you discover you actually value.

Many experienced Bali nomads split their time: 2 weeks in Canggu for community, 2 weeks in Ubud for focus, 1 week somewhere quiet to reset. You don't have to pick one.

---

**Bottom line:** Canggu is the easiest place to land — but not necessarily the best place to stay. Bali has distinct regions for different lifestyles. Try at least two before you decide where you belong.
